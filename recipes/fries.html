French Fries

French fries, also known as chips, are a popular snack or side dish made from deep-fried potatoes. Originating in Belgium in the late 17th century, they quickly spread to other countries and became a staple in fast-food restaurants around the world. The process of making French fries involves cutting potatoes into thin, elongated strips and deep-frying them until they are crispy and golden brown. The fries are typically served with salt and a variety of condiments such as ketchup, mayonnaise, or vinegar. French fries can be made in a variety of ways, including oven-baked, air-fried, or deep-fried. They are often enjoyed as a comfort food and are a popular accompaniment to hamburgers, hot dogs, and sandwiches. While they are not the healthiest of foods due to their high fat and calorie content, French fries remain a beloved treat for many people around the world.

Ingredients

  • Potatoes (preferably Russet or Idaho)
  • Vegetable oil (or other frying oil of your choice)
  • Salt
  • Optional: other seasonings or dipping sauces of your choice (such as ketchup or garlic aioli)

Preparation

Peel the potatoes and cut them into thin, even strips. You can use a mandolin or a sharp knife to achieve uniform thickness.
Soak the potatoes in a bowl of cold water for 30 minutes. This will remove excess starch and help them crisp up when fried.
Drain the potatoes and pat them dry with a clean towel.
Heat the oil in a deep fryer or a large pot over medium-high heat. The oil should be hot enough to fry the potatoes but not so hot that it smokes.
Add a small batch of potatoes to the hot oil and fry until golden brown and crispy. Use a slotted spoon or a wire mesh skimmer to remove them from the oil and place them on a paper towel-lined plate to drain excess oil. Repeat until all the potatoes are cooked.
Season the fries with salt and any other desired spices, such as garlic powder or paprika.
Serve the French fries hot with your favorite dipping sauce, such as ketchup, mayonnaise, or aioli. Enjoy!
