Pizza

Pizza is a popular dish that originated in Italy and has since become a beloved food around the world. The classic pizza consists of a thin crust made of flour, water, yeast, and salt, topped with tomato sauce and cheese. From there, the toppings can vary widely, with options including meats such as pepperoni or sausage, vegetables like mushrooms or onions, and even fruits like pineapple. The pizza can be baked in a wood-fired oven, a conventional oven, or even on a grill. Pizza is often served as a fast and casual food, but can also be found at high-end restaurants or made at home from scratch. Its versatility and simplicity make it a favorite for many people, and it continues to evolve and adapt to new tastes and trends.

  • Olive Oil
  • Salt
  • Sugar

Toppings

  • Cheese
  • Ham
  • Olives
  • Mushrooms
  • Tomato Sauce
  • Oregano

Preparation

In a large mixing bowl, add 500g of flour, 10g of salt, and 7g of instant yeast.
Mix the dry ingredients together.
Add 325ml of warm water and 50ml of olive oil to the bowl. Mix the ingredients together until a dough forms.
Knead the dough on a floured surface for 10-15 minutes until it becomes smooth and elastic.
Place the dough in a large bowl, cover it with a damp cloth, and let it rise in a warm place for 1-2 hours.
Preheat the oven to 230Â°C.
Cut the risen dough in half and roll each half out on a floured surface to form a circular shape.
Place the rolled-out dough on a baking tray and add your desired toppings.
Bake the pizza in the preheated oven for 10-15 minutes or until the crust is golden brown and the cheese is melted and bubbly.
Remove the pizza from the oven and let it cool for a few minutes before slicing and serving. Enjoy!
